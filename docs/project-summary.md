Song Portrait — Project Summary
The goal is a real-time generative visual experience that translates a Spotify track into a living, breathing piece of audiovisual art. You paste a track, Claude interprets its soul, and a fullscreen shader animation plays — synchronized to the beat, shaped by the song's mood and energy, unique to that song.

The core idea
Every song has a fingerprint — a combination of tempo, energy, emotional valence, key, danceability, acousticness. Most music visualizers treat these as afterthoughts, mapping them to generic equalizer bars or spinning geometry. This project does the opposite: the data is the art. Claude reads the fingerprint and makes curatorial decisions — which colors, which base form, how much chaos — and the shader brings those decisions to life in a way that feels genuinely synesthetic. Like you're seeing the song rather than watching a reaction to it.

The visual language
The base aesthetic is dark, self-luminous, and organic. Forms glow from within against black. Edges are soft and diffuse — no hard lines anywhere. Color is spectral, cycling through prismatic bands the way light refracts through a biological membrane. The overall feel sits somewhere between a thermal scan of a living organism and light passing through a strange lens.
The base form is radially symmetric — a breathing, pulsing organism centered on screen. Energy distorts that symmetry. A calm, low-energy track stays close to its centered form, slow and meditative. A high-energy track tears the symmetry apart — the form stretches, shears, develops tendrils, becomes directional and chaotic. The organism is recognizably the same creature across both states, just agitated differently.
Beat sync is the heartbeat of the whole thing. Every beat sends a shockwave through the form — a radial pulse that ripples outward from the center, distorting the noise field for a fraction of a second before it settles back. At high tempo this feels frenetic. At low tempo it feels like a deep, slow breath.

What Claude contributes
Claude is not just a data pipe. For each song it reasons about the track holistically — artist, title, audio features together — and outputs a set of visual parameters that no fixed algorithm would produce. Which part of the spectrum to anchor the palette in. How far toward chaos the energy distortion should push. Whether the organism breathes slowly or twitches. A short poetic interpretation that appears as a caption. The result is that two songs with similar BPMs but different emotional characters look completely different, in ways that feel considered rather than procedural.

The experience
You open the app. You paste a Spotify track URL. There's a brief moment — Claude thinking — then the screen goes dark and the organism emerges. It lives for as long as you let it run. The beat pulses through it continuously. You can paste a new track and watch the organism transform: colors shift, the form morphs, the pulse frequency changes. No two songs produce the same portrait.
The secondary output is a still — a single frame capture you can save. The organism frozen mid-breath, like a specimen photograph.
